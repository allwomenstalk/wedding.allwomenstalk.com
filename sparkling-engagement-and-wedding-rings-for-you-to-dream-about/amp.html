Frequently Asked Questions

What styles of engagement rings are popular right now?
Popular styles include solitaire, halo, vintage, and modern geometric designs.
How do I choose the right wedding ring to match my engagement ring?
Consider matching the metal type and style for a cohesive look, or opt for a contrasting design to showcase both rings.
What are the most durable metals for wedding rings?
Platinum and palladium are very durable, while gold alloys with higher karat pure gold are softer but still popular.
Are there alternative gemstones besides diamonds for engagement rings?
Yes, sapphires, emeralds, and rubies are beautiful alternatives that add unique color and personality.
How can I make my engagement ring unique?
Personalize it with custom engraving, a rare gemstone, or a bespoke design that reflects your style.
What’s the average budget I should expect for an engagement ring?
Most people spend between 3-6 months of their salary, but the best ring is one that feels right for your budget.
Can I wear my wedding and engagement rings on the same finger?
Yes, traditionally both are worn on the ring finger of the left hand for convenience and symbolism.
How do I care for and clean my engagement and wedding rings?
Regularly clean with mild soap and water, and have them professionally inspected annually for damage or loose stones.
When should I buy my wedding ring relative to the wedding date?
It’s best to buy your wedding ring at least a month before to allow time for sizing and any customizations.
What trends are shaping wedding ring designs for women?
Minimalist bands, mixed metals, and nature-inspired motifs are trending among modern brides.
