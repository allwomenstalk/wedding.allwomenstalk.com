# 9 Beautiful Blush Wedding Dresses ...

Blush wedding dresses have been a huge trend in bridal fashion throughout the past several years, and with so many gorgeous options to choose from, it’s become easier than ever to break with tradition and find a unique dress that’s perfect for your big day. From the soft and romantic to the chic and trendy, here are nine beautiful blush wedding dresses that have definitely caught my eye. Take a look, and let me know if you also love them just as much.

## 1. Garden Romance

![Garden Romance](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/wedding/2013/06/1_garden-romance_300x408.jpg)

Ready for romance? Perfect for a garden wedding during the spring or summer seasons, the soft, light flow of this Carol Hannah gown makes it by far one of my favorite blush wedding dresses. What do you think? Do you love its gentle, breezy look, too?

## 2. Dramatic Ruffles

![Dramatic Ruffles](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/wedding/2013/06/2_dramatic-ruffles_450x600.jpg)

With dramatic layers of ruffles and stunning strings of rosettes, this beautiful blush gown by Moonlight Bridal reminds me of a flamenco dancer’s costume. From the trendy one shouldered-design to the flattering fit-and-flare silhouette, there’s honestly nothing I don’t love about it.

## 3. Colorful Ombre

![Colorful Ombre](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/wedding/2013/06/3_colorful-ombre_493x744.jpg)

Ombre has taken the fashion world by storm in recent years, so why not make it a part of your big day by stepping out in a gorgeous blush ombre gown like this one by White by Vera Wang for David’s Bridal? With its lovely combination of colors and timeless ball gown silhouette, this dress manages to be both trendy and romantic all at once.

## 4. Bold Beauty

![Bold Beauty](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/wedding/2013/06/4_bold-beauty_550x787.jpg)

There’s nothing quite like a single bold detail to add a special touch to an otherwise simple dress, as seen here on this stunning blush gown by Papilio. What do you think of the oversized rosette? Would you wear a dress like this?

## 5. Feminine Flair

![Feminine Flair](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/wedding/2013/06/5_feminine-flair_493x744.jpg)

After first discovering this gorgeous Monique Lhuillier gown, I couldn’t quite make up my mind which I liked better – the corset-style bodice or the fun, flouncy skirt. Luckily, it doesn’t really matter, since the dress perfectly combines both details for a look that’s entirely unique.

## 6. Regal Rosettes

![Regal Rosettes](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/wedding/2013/06/6_regal-rosettes_550x828.jpg)

Hoping to channel your inner princess throughout the day? You can in a stunning blush gown like this one by Sareh Nouri, which evokes a vibe that’s all at once romantic and regal.

## 7. Whimsical FloWERS

![Whimsical FloWERS](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/wedding/2013/06/7_whimsical-flowers_460x680.jpg)

Offering yet another creative twist on the floral look, this whimsical gown by Wtoo is pretty and sweet without coming across as being better suited for the flower girl. The strapless cut also adds a classic and flattering touch.

## 8. Simple Elegance

![Simple Elegance](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/wedding/2013/06/8_simple-elegance_493x744.jpg)

With its simple shift silhouette and high neckline, this modest dress by Rivini strikes me as being remarkably elegant and unfussy. I especially love the pretty floral pattern, along with the gown’s subtle sparkle and chic sleeves.

## 9. Trendy Fringe

![Trendy Fringe](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/wedding/2013/06/9_trendy-fringe_501x648.jpg)

Last but not least, this blush Novia d'Art gown is yet another one of my all-time favorites. From the two-toned bodice to the fringe skirt, it strikes me as being a bit more trendy and fun than some of the more romantic, girly styles I’ve seen. I also think that while the green sash does add an unexpected touch, I would probably skip it. Do you agree, or do you think the green actually provides a nice contrast?

What are some of your own favorite blush wedding dresses? Would you consider wearing one, or do you prefer classic white or even another colorful shade?
