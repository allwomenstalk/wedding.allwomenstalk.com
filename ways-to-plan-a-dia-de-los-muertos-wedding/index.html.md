# 7 Vibrant Ways to Plan a Day of the Dead Wedding: The Ultimate Guide

Dia de Los Muertos is a vibrant Mexican holiday celebration that honors loved ones who have passed away. Falling on November 1st—the day after Halloween and before All Souls' Day—this tradition is anything but somber. A **Dia de Los Muertos wedding theme** is exploding with color, creative skull details, and decor that celebrates life. Whether you are getting married on the Day of the Dead or simply love the aesthetic, here are 7 ways to plan a Dia de Los Muertos wedding that honors tradition with style.

## 1. Dia De Los Muertos Wedding Invitations

![Dia De Los Muertos Wedding Invitations](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/wedding/2012/10/dayofthedeadinvitation_526x737.jpg)

Photo Credit: [Vintagebabydoll](https://www.etsy.com)

These gorgeous paper picado banners are a sign of celebration in Mexico and many other countries. They are intricately detailed with designs of flowers, hearts, and skulls. This colorful paper picado banner invitation with its personalized wedding date will be the perfect way to announce your theme. If you're looking for more [wedding planning tips](https://wedding.allwomenstalk.com/wedding-planning-tips/), starting with cohesive stationery is key.

## 2. Dia De Los Muertos Wedding Makeup

![Dia De Los Muertos Wedding Makeup](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/wedding/2012/10/dayofthedeadwedding_527x795.jpg)

Photo Credit: [Urban Shutter Bug Photography](http://www.urbanshutterbug.com) via [Rock n Roll Bride](https://www.rocknrollbride.com/2011/02/a-dia-de-los-muertos-bridal-shoot/)

If you are a fan of dramatic makeup and are looking for a unique way to bring your offbeat style alive on your wedding day, you're in luck because Dia de Los Muertos has its own iconic makeup look. While this look isn't for everyone, you might wait until your reception to reveal a painted skull face so your guests aren't too shocked during the ceremony. I love this look and think it really brings the theme alive. Check out this [tutorial video](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KUnol-y0mfU) to get great ideas for your big day!

## 3. Dia De Los Muertos Wedding Cake

![Dia De Los Muertos Wedding Cake](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/wedding/2012/10/dayofthedeadcake_530x795.jpg)

Photo Credit: [Rockabelle Bombshell](https://goo.gl/hhoqBZ)

I love that among the skulls in a **Dia de Los Muertos wedding theme** there are plenty of flowers and colorful details as well. This holiday is not about being sad or gloomy; it’s a celebration of life, love, and the happiness you shared with those who have passed. Take inspiration from a stunning Day of the Dead wedding cake and remember the good times!

| Tradition Element | Symbolic Meaning | Modern Wedding Twist |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Sugar Skulls | Remembrance of souls | Personalized guest favors |
| Marigolds | Path for the spirits | Vibrant floral arches |
| Papel Picado | The wind and life | Hanging reception decor |

Integrating these elements ensures your celebration remains authentic while looking spectacular for your guests.

## 4. Dia De Los Muertos Wedding Bouquet

_ Photo Credit: Hazelton Photography_ via _PoptasticBride_

Add a little Dia de Los Muertos fun into your wedding bouquet with skull buttons and pins on your flowers and your bouquet's stem handle. These little details will make your wedding bouquet stand out amongst the rest. If you love this theme but aren't crazy about having head-to-toe skull decor, go with smaller details like this rather than a full-scale skull cake.

## 5. Dia De Los Muertos Wedding Boutonniere

_ Photo Credit: [Melanie Duerkopp Photography](https://melanieduerkopp.com/) via HWTM_

Speaking of small details, how sweet is this Dia de Los Muertos boutonniere! With a guitar pick and a button flower, this is one quirky accessory that blends beautifully with the theme. If you are looking for ways to incorporate the theme without going overboard, you must check out this [bridal makeup ideas](https://wedding.allwomenstalk.com/bridal-makeup-ideas/) and accessory guide for more inspiration. Bonus: the bride looks adorable in her vintage-inspired tea-length dress and blue heels!

## 6. Dia De Los Muertos Bridal Jewelry

![Dia De Los Muertos Bridal Jewelry](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/wedding/2012/10/skullbracelet_527x703.jpg)

Photo Credit: [Gypsy Warrior](https://www.gypsywarrior.com)

I love skull jewelry, especially when it comes to arm candy like a skull beaded bracelet. Whether you are DIY savvy or prefer to shop online, there are plenty of skull bracelets, rings, and necklaces you can find for yourself and your bridesmaids. Here are a few ways to style your accessories:

- Stack beaded skull bracelets for a boho look.
- Choose silver skull studs for a subtle nod to the theme.
- Incorporate colorful gemstones to match the festival vibe.
- Custom engraved rings with traditional motifs.

These pieces serve as wonderful keepsakes long after the wedding day is over.

## 7. Dia De Los Muertos Wedding Favors

![Dia De Los Muertos Wedding Favors](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/wedding/2012/10/dayofthedeadfavors_532x417.jpg)

Photo Credit: [SweetSugarBelle](https://www.sweetsugarbelle.com/2011/10/el-dia-de-los-muertos-day-of-the-dead-cookies/)

In a **Dia de Los Muertos wedding theme**, skulls are smiling and so are your guests as they take home these adorable cookies! This is one of the reasons I love this holiday—it takes a sad moment and turns it into a celebration. Don't just remember the loss; celebrate their life while dancing, drinking, and eating in their honor!

This is not a theme that has to be filled with skulls to get the point across; it's about what the day means to you and bringing the memories of your loved ones to life. Remember to be playful and have lots of colorful details like the paper picado banners to keep with the happiness of the celebration. Is this a theme you are considering for your big day?
